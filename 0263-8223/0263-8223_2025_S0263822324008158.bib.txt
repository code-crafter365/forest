abstract = {Thermoplastic based composites (TPC) have emerged as a new generation of eco-friendly materials with tougher matrices capable of overcoming the major weaknesses of the thermoset counterparts related with poor resistance to delamination and recycling difficulties. Although TPC materials present low surface energy, adhesive bonding is still effective when the requirements for fusion bonding procedures are not met. Recent advances in adhesive technology have unveiled two-part acrylic adhesive specifically designed for low energy surfaces, characteristic of TPC materials. In this work, unidirectional carbon-reinforced polyamide 6 (CF/PA6) bonded joint was characterized under pure mode II loading using end-notched flexure (ENF) test. The experimental fracture tests revealed unstable crack propagation and a data reduction scheme based on the equivalent crack concept was developed to obtain the strain energy release rate distribution along the crack front for the specimenâ€™s length beyond the actuator central loading point. The proposed procedure was successfully validated using a finite element analysis including a cohesive zone modelling and applied to the experimental results. The obtained Resistance-curves showed that this adhesive is capable to provide a significant bonding resistance in pure mode II loading even in low energy surfaces characteristic of TPC materials.}

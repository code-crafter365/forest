This study develops a numerical framework to simulate bulk and interfacial fractures in composite materials. A phase-field cohesive zone model, incorporating a directional energy decomposition scheme and a modified toughness method, is employed to capture complex fracture behaviors. A level-set method explicitly defines interface positions, while an adaptive mesh refinement strategy enhances computational efficiency. Numerical examples validate the modelâ€™s accuracy and efficiency in predicting mixed-mode crack propagation and interfacial debonding.

The proposed method is demonstrated on benchmark problems of minimum compliance and heat transfer, demonstrating its ability to yield crisp optimized designs while adhering to length scale requirements. Numerical results show that CAMP outperforms traditional constraint-aggregation methods, offering superior design performance and improved computational efficiency.

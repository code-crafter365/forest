abstract = {Preparing large-sized, high-performance carbon fiber reinforced aluminum matrix (CF/Al) composite ring components presents significant challenges in the aerospace industry. This study introduces an innovative fabrication method based on indirect extrusion (IE) for CF/Al composite ring components. The influence of the preform structure on the molding quality was investigated, elucidating the alloy liquid infiltration mechanism during the IE process. Additionally, the mechanical properties and failure mechanisms were systematically analyzed. The results showed that the IE composites had better forming quality with a 15% increase in tensile strength and a 17% increase in shear strength compared to composites prepared by direct extrusion (DE). In addition, based on the area calculation under the stressâ€“strain curve, the tensile and shear energy absorption capacities of IE composites were improved by 98% and 73%, respectively.

abstract = {Thin-walled multiway carbon fiber composite tubes have been widely used in spacecraft connectors and satellite trusses. However, stitching and splicing techniques are commonly employed to fabricate such special-shaped structures with stress concentration at joining regions, leading to inferior long-term performance. In this study, a yarn reorganization strategy is proposed to develop a 3D braiding technique for braiding seamless four-way tubes. The prepared four-way composite tubes exhibited typical advantageous properties including structural integrity and improved mechanical properties. The axial compression evaluation revealed that the structureâ€™s maximum load-bearing capacity and its initial stiffness decreased as branch length increased. An increase in branch length also heightened the susceptibility to buckling under axial compression, thereby reducing the overall load-bearing capacity of the structure. Axial compression ultimately caused the axial branch width to expand by 105%, while the lateral branch width was compressed to 88%. The yarn reorganization strategy proposed in this study has significant advantages in the fabrication of high-performance 3D braided composites for aerospace and automotive.}
